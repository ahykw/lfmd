Abstract

We propose LF-MultiDiffusion, a training-free panorama generation method that extends MultiDiffusion to support arbitrary linear projections between target and reference image spaces. Our key idea is to reformulate latent aggregation as a regularized least-squares problem. Its solution is no longer a simple weighted average, making explicit inversion computationally impractical. We therefore introduce a Krylov-based iterative solver into the denoising loop. Compared with prior training-free panorama methods that rely on direct pixel sampling between panorama and perspective images, our formulation enables denser and more natural mappings, yielding more stable generation with far fewer perspective views. This reduces the number of image generator evaluations during denoising and significantly improves inference efficiency. Experiments show that our method achieves better visual quality, text alignment, and geometric consistency while achieving 15× speedup over existing training-free baselines.
